The Eohippus

This was the first form of the horse it was called the Eohippus it lived about 40-50 million years ago. t is very different from the horse that we know today. It was only about 24 inches tall and had a curved back along with a much smaller snout and it’s teeth weren't as developed as todays horse. It was a small forest animal that was much like a modern day deer in the way it moved but looked most like a dog.

The Mesohippus

This early evolution of the horse lived about 35 million years ago. It is still very different from a horse but much has changed since the times of the Eohippus, it is now about 24 inches tall and has longer legs and a straighter back that resembles the one of today's horses. Both its neck and snout are longer and has only 3 toes on each feet instead of 4.

The Miohippus

This species appeared about 36 million years ago. It is very similar to the Mesohippus. The only changes between the two are that it now has a bigger skull with a big crest and it’s ankle joints are now much different.

The Marychippus

This formed about 17 millions years ago and begins to take the shape of the horse be know today. It is now about 40 inches tall and developed larger teeth roots that accommodated it’s bigger skull. The brain got larger and more complex allowing the horse to become more agile.

The Pliohpippus

This species formed about 15 million years ago. The horse didn't evolve from this species but is still part of the evolutionary tree. It’s snout has a deep facial fossae while the horse doesn't. It also had curved teeth which are different front straight modern day horses straight teeth.

The Eqquis

This is the modern day horse it is about 52 inches high. The leg bones fused together and ligaments formed to stop twisting of the hooves. It nows has much longer legs, neck, spine, and skull than the original Eohippus. It’s brain also developed more to make it smarter.

What is the Law of Superposition?

The law of superposition is the basis that the deeper something is in the earth the older it is, this is used by scientist to determine when the species lived. This happens because as time goes nature and gravity begin to compress the old rock and put new rock on top of it.

Evidence of evolution through DNA

DNA between different species can be used to prove how closely related two species are two each other. Depending on how similar the DNA of a species is to another we can determine when the two species diverged from one another in the evolutionary tree depending on how much or how little they relate to each other. An example of this is between humans and chimpanzees, by looking at the DNA of both species all but about 1.2% of the genes are similar so this leads us to believe that humans and chimpanzees diverged as a species not to long ago.

Evidence of evolution through structure

Many species today have similarities within their anatomy, this is another example of evolution. An example of this occurring are the 3 common bones (radius, humorous, and the ulna) in the arm of many species, including humans, whales, birds, and reptiles. Scientist use this as evidence of evolution.
